Hub Overview

Houston George Bush Intercontinental is United Airlines' third-largest hub and its primary gateway to Latin America and the Caribbean. With approximately 470 daily departures, IAH offers the deepest network of Central and South American routes of any US airline hub.

United operates from Terminals B and C/E. Terminal C is the primary international terminal with Global First and Polaris lounges. Terminal E handles additional international and domestic flights. The Skyway automated train connects all terminals.

Delay Patterns at IAH

Houston's Gulf Coast climate creates distinct delay patterns throughout the year:

Spring & Summer (Apr‚ÄìSep)

Thunderstorms are the dominant delay cause. Gulf moisture fuels intense afternoon convective activity, often triggering ground stops. Houston is one of the most lightning-prone metro areas in the US. Afternoon departures (2‚Äì7 PM) are highest risk.

Hurricane Season (Jun‚ÄìNov)

Tropical systems can cause multi-day disruptions. Even storms that don't directly hit Houston create extended flow constraints and preemptive cancellations. IAH has comprehensive hurricane preparedness protocols.

Winter (Dec‚ÄìFeb)

Dense morning fog is common in Houston, occasionally reducing visibility to near-zero. This can delay early morning departures by 1‚Äì2 hours. Ice storms are rare but devastating when they occur (as Houston lacks de-icing infrastructure for extreme events).

Tip: For summer travel through IAH, book the earliest departure possible. Thunderstorm risk increases dramatically after noon and cascading delays compound through the evening.
