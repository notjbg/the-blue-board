Hub Overview

Denver International Airport is United Airlines' second-largest hub and its fastest-growing operation. With approximately 500 daily departures, DEN serves as the critical mid-continent connecting point, linking the East and West Coasts with efficient one-stop itineraries.

United operates exclusively from Concourse B, the largest of DEN's three concourses, accessed via the underground automated train from Jeppesen Terminal. United Club lounges are located at both the east and west ends of Concourse B.

Delay Patterns at DEN

Denver's position on the Colorado Front Range creates unique weather challenges. The airport handles it better than most thanks to its 6-runway layout, but disruptions are frequent:

Summer (Jun‚ÄìAug)

Afternoon thunderstorms are nearly daily occurrences along the Front Range. Convective activity typically builds between 2‚Äì6 PM, triggering ground stops and GDPs. Morning flights have significantly better on-time rates.

Winter (Nov‚ÄìMar)

Blizzards and heavy snow can close runways for hours. DEN's snow removal operations are among the best in the country, but major storms (common in March) can cause widespread cancellations. De-icing delays of 30‚Äì60 minutes are routine in cold weather.

Year-Round

Wind shear and microbursts from mountain wave turbulence can force runway configuration changes. DEN's wide runway spacing provides resilience, but high-wind days reduce throughput.

Tip: Book morning departures from DEN whenever possible. Afternoon thunderstorms in summer are almost guaranteed, and winter storms tend to worsen through the day.
