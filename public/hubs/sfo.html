Hub Overview

San Francisco International is United Airlines' headquarters hub and the primary gateway for transpacific flights to Asia and Oceania. With approximately 300 daily departures, SFO is the nerve center of United's Pacific strategy and a key tech industry travel hub.

United operates from Terminal 3 (domestic) and International Terminal G gates (long-haul). The Polaris Lounge at SFO is one of the best in the system. United's corporate headquarters are located adjacent to the airport in the Willis Tower complex.

Delay Patterns at SFO

SFO has a unique delay profile driven almost entirely by one factor: fog.

The Fog Problem (May‚ÄìAug)

San Francisco's famous marine layer is the dominant operational challenge. SFO's two closely-spaced parallel runways (28L/28R) require 4,000+ feet visibility for simultaneous approaches. When the fog rolls in ‚Äî and it does, frequently ‚Äî the airport drops to single-runway operations, cutting arrival capacity roughly in half. This triggers ground delay programs that ripple nationwide.

Summer Irony

Unlike most airports where summer means thunderstorm delays, SFO's worst months are June and July ‚Äî peak fog season. Mark Twain's (misattributed) quote about San Francisco's cold summers applies directly to airport operations.

Winter (Nov‚ÄìMar)

Atmospheric rivers and heavy rain can reduce visibility and create turbulence on approach. These events are less frequent than fog but can cause more severe multi-hour disruptions when they hit.

Year-Round

Wind shifts requiring runway configuration changes can cause brief delays. SFO's bayside location means crosswinds are common but usually manageable.

Tip: For summer travel through SFO, book morning departures before 10 AM ‚Äî fog typically burns off by early afternoon but the GDP damage is already done. Afternoon flights often carry cumulative delays from the morning fog.

Starlink WiFi at SFO

As United's HQ hub, SFO sees a wide mix of aircraft. Starlink satellite internet is being installed on narrowbody domestic aircraft first (737 MAX), with widebody transpacific aircraft (787, 777) following. Given the long over-water flights to Asia, Starlink will be transformative for the SFO-based Pacific routes.

Frequently Asked Questions

Check the live panel above. SFO delays are heavily weather-dependent ‚Äî clear days run smoothly, fog days don't. Open SFO on The Blue Board for flight-level detail.

What terminal is United at SFO?

Domestic flights: Terminal 3. International flights: International Terminal (G gates). Polaris Lounge is in the International Terminal. BART connects directly to the airport.

Why does SFO have so many fog delays?

SFO's closely-spaced runways can't be used simultaneously in low visibility. The marine layer frequently drops visibility below minimums, forcing single-runway ops and halving capacity. This is worst May through August.
