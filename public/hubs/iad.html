Hub Overview

Washington Dulles International is United Airlines' DC-area hub, serving the capital region's government, military, and business travel markets. With approximately 250 daily departures, IAD provides both domestic connectivity and a growing international network.

United operates from Concourses C and D, connected by the AeroTrain from the iconic Eero Saarinen-designed main terminal. United Club lounges are in both concourses. The Silver Line Metro now connects IAD directly to downtown DC.

Delay Patterns at IAD

IAD benefits from more runway capacity and less airspace congestion than Newark, but shares the same East Coast weather vulnerability:

Summer (Jun‚ÄìAug)

Thunderstorms along the I-95 corridor are the biggest delay driver. Convective weather affects the entire mid-Atlantic region, often simultaneously impacting IAD, EWR, and the New York airports. Afternoon departures (2‚Äì7 PM) carry the highest delay risk.

Winter (Dec‚ÄìFeb)

Snow and ice storms can significantly impact operations. The DC area handles winter weather less efficiently than northern airports ‚Äî a few inches of snow can cause cascading ground transportation issues that compound airport delays. Nor'easters are the worst-case scenario.

Year-Round

IAD generally outperforms EWR on on-time metrics thanks to better runway separation and less airspace congestion. It's the more reliable of United's two East Coast hubs.

Tip: If you have a choice between connecting through EWR or IAD, IAD is typically more reliable ‚Äî especially in summer. Less airspace congestion means faster recovery from weather events.

Starlink WiFi at IAD

United is rolling out Starlink satellite internet across its fleet. IAD's mix of narrowbody and widebody aircraft means availability varies by route. Domestic 737 MAX flights are getting Starlink first.
