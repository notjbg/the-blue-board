Hub Overview

Newark Liberty International is United Airlines' East Coast flagship hub and the primary transatlantic gateway. With approximately 400 daily departures, EWR is the airline's most important premium revenue hub, generating disproportionate international and business travel income.

United operates from Terminal C, which has been significantly renovated and expanded. United Club and Polaris Lounge facilities are among the best in the system. The AirTrain connects to NJ Transit and Amtrak for direct rail access to Manhattan (Penn Station, ~25 minutes).

Delay Patterns at EWR

Newark is consistently the most delay-prone major airport in the United States. The challenges are both structural and environmental:

Airspace Congestion (Year-Round)

EWR shares the New York TRACON airspace with JFK and LaGuardia ‚Äî the busiest and most complex airspace in the world. FAA flow restrictions at any of the three airports cascade to the others. Even on clear days, sequencing constraints limit EWR throughput.

Runway Configuration

EWR's two closely-spaced parallel runways cannot be used independently in low visibility. When the airport drops to single-runway operations (common in rain, fog, or low clouds), arrival rates are cut roughly in half, triggering immediate GDPs.

Summer (Jun‚ÄìAug)

Thunderstorms along the I-95 corridor cause the most severe disruptions. Convective weather can close the entire New York airspace for hours. Multi-hour ground stops are not uncommon, and recovery can take the rest of the day.

Winter (Dec‚ÄìFeb)

Snow, ice, and nor'easters can shut down operations entirely. De-icing delays are routine, and EWR's limited gate space means aircraft backups compound quickly.

Tip: If you're connecting through EWR, build in extra time ‚Äî especially in summer. A 2-hour connection that looks fine on paper can easily be missed during a ground stop. Morning departures have the best odds, but EWR can go sideways at any time.

Frequently Asked Questions

What terminal is United at Newark?

Terminal C. United Club and Polaris Lounge are both in Terminal C. AirTrain connects to all terminals and the rail station.

Why is Newark airport always delayed?

Closely-spaced runways + the world's most congested airspace (shared with JFK and LGA) + East Coast weather. Even moderate weather triggers ground delay programs because capacity margins are razor-thin.
